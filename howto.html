How to Solve the Cube

Understanding the CFOP Method and using the AI Cube Trainer.

Step 1 The Cross

The standard way to begin solving a Rubik's cube is by creating a "Cross" on the bottom layer (usually the white face). You must align four edge pieces around the center piece, ensuring the side colors of those edges match the center pieces of the adjacent faces.

Step 2 First Two Layers (F2L)

Instead of solving the cube one layer at a time, modern speedcubers solve the first two layers simultaneously. This involves finding a corner piece and its matching edge piece, pairing them up in the top layer, and inserting them together into their correct slot in the bottom two layers.

Step 3 OLL (Orientation of the Last Layer)

Once the first two layers are done, your goal is to make the entire top face one solid color (usually yellow), regardless of where the side pieces end up. There are 57 different possible patterns you can encounter here.

How to use this trainer:

  1. Hold your cube so the unsolved layer (Yellow) is facing up.
  2. Look at the top face of your cube. Tap the grid on the screen to match the exact yellow sticker layout you see.
  3. If the top face isn't enough to identify the algorithm, the app will ask you to fill out the side stickers (the 20-bit mask) to find the exact match.
  4. Look at the Rotation angle on the screen to ensure you are holding the cube correctly, then execute the provided algorithm!

Step 4 PLL (Permutation of the Last Layer)

Now that the entire top face is yellow, the final step is to move those yellow pieces around until the sides line up and the cube is completely solved. There are 21 different PLL algorithms.
